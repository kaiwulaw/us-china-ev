Li Mou v. BYD Auto Co., Ltd. — lemon law claim, range degradation after OTA software update
China
Court: Shenzhen Nanshan District People's Court Filed: Oct 31, 2025 Decided: Feb 6, 2026 Decided — plaintiff awarded

Plaintiff alleged that BYD's OTA update (v2.3.1, released Sep 2025) reduced the Han EV's rated range by 18% without consumer consent or adequate disclosure, constituting a product defect under the Three-Package Rules for Automobiles. Court awarded full refund plus 10% punitive supplement under the Consumer Rights Protection Law Art. 55.

Chen v. SAIC-GM-Wuling Automobile — airbag non-deployment, Hongguang MINI EV
China
Court: Liuzhou Intermediate People's Court Filed: Aug 22, 2025 Decided: Jan 10, 2026 Decided — defendant liable

Plaintiff sustained serious injuries in a frontal collision when airbags failed to deploy in the Hongguang MINI EV at speeds triggering deployment per specifications. Court found product defect under the Product Quality Law Art. 41, ordering compensation of ¥340,000. Defendant has appealed to the Guangxi Higher People's Court.
